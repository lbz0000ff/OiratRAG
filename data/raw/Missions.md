Missions are tasks formatted as a "tree", with each completed task allowing the completion of another task in addition to a reward. There are manygeneric missionsavailable to all nations, with others being available depending upon nation, culture, religion, or region.

Missions are assigned whenever certain triggers happen, usually when starting the game or forming another nation. Whenever such a trigger happens the game checks the files for all missions the nations currently fulfills the conditions for and makes them available in the missions tab.

Furthermore, Mission trees in the files are seperated into multiple sections, with every section occupying a dedicated slot in the mission tree (divided in rows and columns and assigend via coordinates with x being rows and y being columns). Each section may have its own conditions to be available to a nation. Thus a single nation may have missions from multiple files and not always all missions from a single file.

If a nation qualifies for multiple sections that apply the same slot in the tree, the missions take priority equal to their order (alphabetical) in the files. Missions that are lower down have higher priority and will replace missions from further up. This is why "generic missions" are called "00_Generic_missions" in the files; they will always be at the very top and can thus even be replaced by other generic missions.
